Meet the candidates for Graduate Students' Association 2025

Hridesh Kapur - GSA President

I'm Hridesh Kapur, and I'm running for GSA President because I firmly believe that the GSA is FOR the students. This means fostering open communication, transparency, and a platform where every graduate student feels heard and valued. I'm committed to improving the connection between GSA and students, ensuring that your concerns, ideas, and feedback are always a priority.

Having organized events like Holi, Nowruz, and Lunar New Year, among others, I've worked to create spaces where all cultures and nationalities are celebrated. I bring an inclusive mindset that values diversity and aims to make every graduate student feel like they truly belong at Carleton.

If elected, I will focus on creating an environment where communication is clear, decisions are transparent, and the graduate student experience is enriched. Together, we can build a GSA that truly represents and serves you.

Key Platform Points:

  • Better communication and transparency between the GSA and students
  • Make GSA processes clear and accessible - no more vague by-laws
  • Increase awareness of GSA services like free printing, dental/vision insurance, and social events
  • An Open, Informed GSA for Every Student

Hadi Hefzy - GSA Vice-President External

Hi! I'm Hadi Hefzy (Behnam), and I'm running for GSA Vice-President External because I'm passionate about building strong connections, both within and beyond Carleton University. As an international student from Iran (and yes, like any true Iranian, I love engaging with people, whether it's on campus or in the city, I'm always ready to listen and help), I truly understand the challenges international students face and I'm eager to represent and support them.

I'm an active, outgoing, and communication-driven person who believes that real change comes through meaningful dialogue. If elected, I will work to strengthen GSA's relationships with external organizations, improve outreach, and ensure every student, especially international graduate students - feels seen, heard, and included.

Key Platform Points:

  • Strengthen GSA's communication with external bodies, including local governments and student associations
  • Be a strong and reliable voice for international graduate students
  • Improve outreach and raise awareness about GSA services and activities
  • Support deeper collaboration with student groups and service centres on campus
  • Organize traditional and cultural celebrations from various cultures in a broader and more inclusive way

Temuulen Turbat (Elen) - GSA VP Academics

Hi, I'm Temuulen Turbat (Elen), and I'm excited to run for GSA VP Academics to advocate for graduate students. Originally from the steppes of Mongolia, I believe every graduate student deserves strong support, accessible resources, and real opportunities to succeed. The GSA should be a safe, inclusive, and empowering space, regardless of background, gender, or identity.

As a core member of the Mongolia Inclusion & Diversity group, I've led initiatives to promote fair workplaces, secure funding for social causes, and advocate for equity. Now, I want to bring that same dedication to the GSA, ensuring every graduate student feels heard, valued, and supported.

In academia, thriving goes beyond just meeting deadlines. We need a balance that supports our mental health and well-being, along with resources for career development, networking, professional skill-building, and clear, accessible GSA services. As a dog mom, I understand how essential it is to find this balance and to have a supportive, inclusive community to help us succeed.

Key Platform Points:

  • Expand career development programs
  • Ensure graduate students' academic concerns are heard and effectively addressed
  • Advocate for stronger mental health and wellness resources
  • Foster an inclusive academic environment where every student feels represented and valued

Pasca Sugijarto - Graduate Faculty Board

I'm Pasca Sugijarto, I'm running for Graduate Faculty Board and I am happy to work together with Graduate Faculty members to bring forward issues that are close to Carleton Graduate Students.

I come from a small family, from a small city in Indonesia. As a true Indonesian, I love "sambal" and "Indomie". I came to Canada with a small white dog named Bamse. He has been my champion through our first winter and helped me cope with every challenge I met as an international student.

I believe in creating a family wherever I go - celebrate happy milestones, support positive actions, be there for sad moments and share good food. Therefore, I try to hold dear what is good and true because life comes with ups and downs. Friends - who are honest & sincere, who are good & true to others - are the family that will carry each other through those waves of life.

I want to create a supportive and inclusive graduate community where students feel valued, empowered, and connected, like a family. I will advocate for initiatives that foster mentorship, peer support networks, and emotional well-being, ensuring that students have a support system throughout their academic journey.

Key Platform Points:

  • Support initiatives for academic guidance, financial support, and mental health services
  • Support for more openness in GSA processes
  • Oversee community-focused activities
  • Incorporate art as part of stress relief
